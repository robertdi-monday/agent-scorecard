# Agent Auditor — 60-Second Live Intro Script
## (Spoken by Robert before the demo video plays)

> **Format:** Robert speaks live for ~60 seconds → demo video plays  
> **Audience:** CCO, Lior Grunhaus (RVP NAM & LATAM / Head of AI), CS Leadership  
> **Tone:** Confident, practitioner, peer-to-peer — not a pitch

---

## THE SCRIPT

**[0–15s] — The moment we're in**

> "We're at an inflection point. Customers aren't asking *if* they should build agents anymore — they're asking *how many* they should build.
> And our team is often right there in the room helping them do it."

---

**[15–30s] — The gap nobody talks about**

> "But there's a question that comes up every single time, and we haven't had a great answer for it:
> *'How do I know this agent is actually good?'*
> Not just functional — but trustworthy. Safe to deploy. Worth the credits."

---

**[30–48s] — What we built**

> "So I built something to answer that.
> Agent Auditor is an agent that evaluates other agents — before they go live.
> It runs a 4-layer check: scans for vulnerabilities, reviews instruction alignment, verifies output against board data, and generates paste-ready fixes for everything it finds.
> No engineering required."

---

**[48–60s] — Hand off to video**

> "In the next 60 seconds, you'll see it audit a real agent — a CSM account health monitor — go from a low confidence score to medium, with one copied fix.
> Here's the demo."

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

- **Slow down** on "How do I know this agent is *actually* good?" — that's the line that lands.
- "No engineering required" is the CS-specific payoff — pause on it.
- The hand-off line should feel like a movie trailer tease, not a slide transition.
- **Do not rush the opening** — the first 15 seconds are what earns you the next 45.
